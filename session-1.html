What If Technology Fails?

Social Media, Communication and Conditional Thinking

Session 1

Exploring digital life and conditionals

In this session, you will reflect on your use of technology and social media while practising English conditionals through different interactive activities.

My digital habits

Duración
20:00
Pair work

Work in pairs. Start by talking about your digital habits. Answer the following questions about your use of social media and time online and compare your answers in pairs.

  1. How much time do you usually spend online every day?
  2. What social media platforms do you use most often? Why?
  3. Do you check your phone first thing in the morning?
  4. Have you ever felt stressed because of social media?
  5. Do you think you could live without your smartphone for one day? Why / why not?
  6. What is the most positive thing about social media for you?
  7. What is the biggest problem related to social media in your opinion?
  8. Have you ever had a negative experience online? (If you want, you can explain briefly)
  9. Do you think people share too much personal information online?
  10. How do you usually communicate with friends: messages, calls, social media, or in person? Why?

Grammar & discussion: If technology disappeared…

Duración
40:00
Group work

In groups, discuss different situations related to technology and social media. Use conditional sentences to explain what could happen, giving opinions, causes and consequences. You can use the presentations posted in the section Student support as help.

  1. If smartphones disappeared tomorrow, how would your daily life change?
    What would you do differently during the day?
  2. If social media no longer existed, how would people communicate with each other?
    Would life be better or worse?
  3. If the internet stopped working for a week, what problems would people face at work or school?
    How would you solve them?
  4. If online banking disappeared, how would people manage their money?
    What alternatives would they use?
  5. If nobody had access to GPS or maps apps, how would people travel or find places?
    Would it be difficult?
  6. If artificial intelligence tools suddenly stopped working, how would students and workers be affected?
    What tasks would become more difficult?

You find a video that explains the advantages and disadvantages of social media, and you decide to help by summarising the most important information.

Your task:
You must send a voice message in English to your friend explaining the key ideas from the video in a clear and simple way.
